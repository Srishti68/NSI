In Memory of Arunima Sinha

A Life Well Lived

Arunima Sinha's story is one of extraordinary resilience, unwavering determination, and an undying spirit. From humble beginnings in rural India to scaling the world's highest peak as a double amputee, her journey is an inspiration to millions.

Arunima's life took a dramatic turn in 2011 when she was pushed off a moving train by robbers, leading to the amputation of her left leg below the knee. This life-altering event could have easily broken her, but Arunima refused to be defined by her disability. Instead, she discovered an inner strength she never knew she possessed.

Fueled by a newfound purpose and inspired by cricketer Yuvraj Singh's battle against cancer, Arunima set her sights on the seemingly impossible: conquering Mount Everest. Her journey was arduous. She endured grueling training, overcame societal skepticism, and battled physical challenges that would daunt even the most able-bodied individuals.

In 2013, Arunima defied all odds and became the first female amputee to scale Mount Everest, etching her name in history. This wasn't just a personal triumph; it was a beacon of hope for countless individuals facing their own struggles.

Arunima's achievements didn't stop there. She went on to conquer Mount Kilimanjaro, Mount Elbrus, Mount Kosciuszko, Mount Aconcagua, Carstensz Pyramid, and Mount Vinson, becoming the first woman amputee to summit all seven continents' highest peaks.

Beyond her mountaineering feats, Arunima is a passionate advocate for social change. She established the Shaheed Chandra Shekhar Vikalang Khel Academy, a free sports academy for underprivileged and disabled individuals. Through her foundation, she empowers others to overcome challenges and pursue their dreams.

Arunima Sinha's life is a testament to the power of the human spirit. She is an embodiment of courage, perseverance, and the unwavering belief that anything is possible. Her story continues to inspire people worldwide, reminding us that even in the face of adversity, the human spirit can soar to unimaginable heights.
